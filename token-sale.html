Interactive Coin Offering

Why an IICO?
The interactive initial coin offering protocol allows participants to submit an ETH payment for tokens along with a personal valuation cap.

There is no personal cap required but instead a Personal valuation cap. An indication by each participant that they are willing to leave their token purchase in the sale as long as the maximum total sale valuation does not exceed their personal valuation cap.

If, at the very end of the sale, the total sale valuation is greater than their personal valuation cap, the participant’s bid is refunded. If this is still a little confusing take a look at our explainer video below.

How can I get a bonus?
Kleros has not done a token pre-sale. The way to get discounted tokens is to participate early in the token sale. This mechanism allows universal participation but at the same time rewards those who look to maximize their contribution to the sale.
Why multiple rounds?
Our method is inspired by the old method of funding startups through a number of rounds (series A, series B, etc.). Startup teams get access to more resources as they prove they are making progress with their product.

We're trying to bring this old, proven method into the crazy world of token models. We hope this brings peace of mind to our participants that we will deliver the protocol in a timely fashion without the need to ask for huge amounts of investment up front.

F.A.Q

What happen with unsold tokens, if any, after the Token Sale?
There can't be unsold tokens with an interactive coin offering. Tokens are given in proportion of contribution (and taking a potential bonus into account).
Are US citizens allow to participate? If so, how are you fully compliant with US regulation?
US citizens are not allowed to participate.
Who is advising you on the legal side? Who is your legal advisor or group of legal advisors?
We are advised by William O'Rorke, a french lawyer specialized in cryptos and ICOs. He's in charge of the project compliance regarding french and European regulations.
Is there a lock up period for the tokens after the token sale is over?
Not for buyers. Tokens will be usable as soon as the sale ends.
What jurisdiction you will incorporate the organization? What kind of Legal Entity you will use for the Organization?
We will be incorporated in France as a Cooperative.
Do you have a structure where funds are lock up and released upon completion of project milestones?
No, we would have loved to but there isn’t currently a decentralized dispute resolution system which would be able to solve disputes about milestone completion (we are working on it ;) ). The token sale will be in multiple rounds. Would we fail to deliver what is promised, buyers would probably not buy in the next rounds.
